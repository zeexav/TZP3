Relationship between Number of Ratings and Mean Rating

The Scatter Plot above demonstrates a few facts about the data set:

  • Most movies are rated less than 20,000 times
  • The majority of movies have a mean rating of between 3 and 4 on a scale of 1 to 5
  • There is a slight positive relationship between number of ratings and mean rating
  • The movies that are rated 5 have very few ratings

Summary of the Distribution of the Total Ratings per User

The histogram and other supporting exploratory analysis demonstrated the following facts about the Ratings dataset:

  • The majority of users rated less than a 100 movies. Exploratory Data Analysis showed that the mean number of ratings per user is 56.
  • Very few users rated more than 500 movies

Due to limited processing power, we had to limit the size of our Ratings dataset. As a result of these results, we chose to utilize a subset of similar users based on the fact that they rated a similar number of movies. We attempted a variety of ranges to find a quality dataset of users and movies. In a larger version of this project we used Users who rated between 75 and 150 movies.
